Apple-Inspired Evaluation Frame

Choose the direction that best answers the farmer's first question: is the field okay, what changed, how fresh is the data, and what should be checked next?

  • Clarity: readable text, plain actions, precise status labels.
  • Deference: interface surfaces support field content instead of decorating it.
  • Depth: layered cards and motion-ready hierarchy show what is primary.
  • Consistency: familiar mobile navigation, repeated components, predictable states.

Recommended Direction

Use Direction 1 as the base, borrow Direction 3 for alert escalation, Direction 4 for history, and Direction 8 for Demo Mode. Keep the Apple-inspired restraint: fewer competing colors, more whitespace, clear typography, and surfaces that feel quiet until the field state needs attention.

Selected Style: Apple Field Health

This direction follows the reference screens: iPhone-native rhythm, soft off-white background, bright but restrained health green, large readable headings, rounded cards, crop imagery, circular health scoring, and a floating bottom tab bar.

The key Apple principle is restraint. The UI feels premium because it removes noise: one main visual moment, one health summary, then compact sensor evidence. The hierarchy must stay action-first: status, reason, recommended action, then history.

Apple Field Health should feel calm and polished, but never decorative. If the field is critical, the alert beats the photo. If the health score changes, the app explains which reading caused it. If data is stale or offline, the interface says so clearly.

ClarityBig titles, plain labels, short status phrases, and a single primary answer per screen.
DeferenceThe rice field, health score, alert content, and advice are the heroes. Chrome stays quiet.
DepthLayered cards, floating tab bar, circular score, and image cards create hierarchy without clutter.
ConsistencyEvery screen uses the same title scale, pill filters, white cards, green active state, and bottom navigation behavior.
ImplementationBuild as reusable Flutter components: app shell, field hero, health ring, metric cards, alert filters, advice cards, history chart, and settings profile.
